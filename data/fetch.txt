Project: Fetch Rewards UX Research

Type: Mobile App UX Research / Consumer Product

Description:
A UX research project focused on improving the receipt scanning and reward experience in the Fetch Rewards app.

Users:
- Everyday consumers using receipt scanning for rewards
- Mobile app users seeking quick and seamless interactions

Problem:
- Users experienced friction during receipt scanning
- Unclear feedback and system status during upload
- Confusing reward redemption flow
- Lack of clarity in how points are earned and used

My Role:
UX Researcher + Product Designer

Research Methods:
- User interviews
- Usability testing
- Heuristic evaluation
- Behavioral observation

Actions:
- Identified key pain points in the scanning and reward flow
- Analyzed user behavior during receipt upload process
- Redesigned interaction flow to reduce confusion
- Improved feedback and system visibility during scanning
- Simplified reward understanding and redemption process

Key Features / Improvements:
- Clearer scanning feedback (loading, success, failure states)
- Improved onboarding for new users
- Simplified reward points explanation
- More intuitive user flow for earning and redeeming rewards

Impact:
- Reduced user confusion during receipt scanning
- Improved task completion success rate
- Increased overall usability and engagement
- Enhanced clarity of reward system

Skills:
- UX Research
- Usability Testing
- Interaction Design
- User Flow Optimization

Tools:
Figma, User Testing Platforms

Keywords:
mobile app, UX research, usability testing, onboarding, rewards system, user behavior, interaction design

Summary:
I conducted UX research on the Fetch Rewards app and redesigned key parts of the receipt scanning and reward flow to improve usability and user understanding.
